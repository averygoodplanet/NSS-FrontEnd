Apple's iMac Led Quarter Computer Sales

by NYTimes on January 22, 1999

Apple Computer Inc.'s popular iMac desktop computer was the No. 1 selling machine through retail and mail-order channels in the 1998 holiday season, according to the research company PC Data.

In overall sales, the iMac fell from the top spot in November to No. 3 in December. It is still the top-selling computer since its mid-August introduction and led overall sales in the fourth quarter, PC Data, of Reston, Va., reported.

The report comes after Apple stock surged to a 52-week high last week when the company added five colors to the aquiline iMac's range.

The share price then dropped on concern by a Salomon Smith Barney analyst about the pricing of the hot-selling machines. Apple stock closed yesterday at $38.8125, down $1.75.

The iMac accounted for 6.2 percent of all fourth-quarter unit sales and 7.2 percent of the dollar volume, the PC Data report said. It counted the holiday season as October through December 1998.

The average price for the iMac was $1,224 in the fourth quarter, according to PC Data. Apple priced the new iMac color range at $1,199. It cut the price of the original iMac to $1,049, down from an August introductory price of $1,299, and also offered a powerful if more expensive line of Power Mac G3 computers.

Other best sellers in the fourth quarter were three Compaq Computer Corporation Presario PC's. Rounding out the top five was the Hewlett-Packard 6355 computer.

Leading sales in December was the Compaq Presario 5170, followed by the Presario 5150.

After the iMac, the eTower, from EMachines, a privately held supplier of low-cost computers, captured fourth place in December.
